# work/ — what you spend your time on

This directory holds the artifacts of "where most of your time goes".
The actual content varies by person:

| Your role | Roughly what's here |
|-----------|---------------------|
| Student (BA / MA / PhD / vocational) | Course notes, papers, grad-school apps, thesis work, scholarship apps |
| Employed | Resume, target company list, project retros, performance notes, interview notes |
| Freelance / self-employed | Portfolio, client files, contracts, deliverables |
| Founder | Business plan, product docs, team / hiring, fundraising materials |
| Full-time caregiver | Childcare / elder-care logs, related learning materials |
| Retired / gap | Notes from your current main activity (writing / volunteering / learning something new) |

Don't force it. Empty or just one or two of the most relevant files is
plenty.

## What Muse can do with this

- **Student**: polish drafts, grad-school target list advice, interview
  / "cold email" rehearsal, paper feedback, explain hard course concepts
- **Employed**: resume rewrites, target-company intelligence, mock
  interviews, offer negotiation (only on hard contractual terms),
  project pitch
- **Freelance**: client proposal rewrites, pricing advice, portfolio
  ordering, tax thinking
- **Founder**: BP feedback, user-interview retros, hiring JD drafts
- **Caregiver / retired**: gives advice based on your actual daily
  reality, doesn't assume you "should be at work"

## How to organize for best results

- File names with date prefix: `YYYY-MM-xxx.md/pdf`
- The "why I did it this way" matters more than "what I did" — record
  the decision rationale
- Things involving others (coworkers / clients / professors): redact —
  write "M" / "the professor" / "the PM" instead of names

## Getting started

Drop in **one most-relevant** thing:

- Student → latest paper / notes from the course you care most about /
  application materials in progress
- Employed → most recent resume
- Freelance → 1-2 current showcase pieces / current active client list
- Other → notes from whatever takes the largest block of your time
  right now

The first time Muse comes in, it'll ask "which one do you want to start
with?"
